# IELTS Initial Study Plan

## Current Evidence

- One 258-word Task 2 sample was completed in approximately 20-30 minutes.
- The first writing estimate is approximately Band 5.0, but it is not yet a stable band.
- The main constraint in this sample was task interpretation: "free" was understood as
  unrestricted admission rather than free of tuition fees.
- The learner can sustain an essay, attempt complex sentences, and use some abstract
  vocabulary. Current priorities are accurate task interpretation, natural collocations,
  sentence control, and focused argument development.
- Reading, listening, and speaking levels are still unknown.
- The exam is expected to be several months away, so the immediate priority is general
  English development and habit building rather than timed test drilling.
- This version assumes about 60 minutes of study per day and should be adjusted after
  daily study time and diagnostic results are known.

## Week 1: Baseline Diagnosis

Complete the assessment across several days instead of taking a full mock test at once.

| Day | Task | Time | Output |
|---|---|---:|---|
| 1 | Writing sample: IELTS Task 2, untimed | 45-60 min | One original essay |
| 2 | Reading sample | 45-60 min | Answers, time used, and uncertain questions |
| 3 | Listening sample | 40-50 min | Answers plus notes on missed information |
| 4 | Speaking Parts 1 and 2 | 20-30 min | Audio recording or transcript |
| 5 | Vocabulary and grammar review from samples | 45 min | Repeated-error list |
| 6 | Short second sample in the weakest skill | 45-60 min | Comparison sample |
| 7 | Review | 30 min | Baseline summary and next-stage priorities |

Do not use dictionaries, translators, grammar checkers, or AI when producing diagnostic
samples. Record actual time used. One sample is preliminary evidence, not a stable band.

## Weeks 2-9: Foundation Phase

Use this temporary weekly structure until diagnostic evidence supports a more targeted
allocation.

| Frequency | Task | Time | Required output |
|---|---|---:|---|
| 3 days/week | Read an English article or short passage intensively | 25 min | Five useful phrases and a 3-sentence summary |
| 3 days/week | Listen to clear English with transcript checking | 25 min | Five corrected phrases or missed details |
| 2 days/week | Write one paragraph, summary, or essay section | 30 min | Corrected version plus three error notes |
| 3 days/week | Speak on a familiar topic and record it | 10-15 min | One recording and a brief self-review |
| Daily | Review collected vocabulary in context | 10-15 min | Recall result and one original sentence |
| Weekly | Review the error book and redo selected work | 30 min | Before/after comparison |

## Feedback Loop

Every Sunday:

1. Check completed outputs rather than study time alone.
2. Select the three most frequent or damaging errors.
3. Redo one earlier task without looking at its correction.
4. Adjust the following week's skill allocation.

Run a broader reassessment after four weeks. Begin sustained timed IELTS practice only
after the basic routine is stable and an approximate exam window and target band are
known.

## Four-Week Vocabulary Plan

This plan assumes 15 minutes of vocabulary study within the current 60-minute daily
schedule. The initial focus is reliable use of common collocations rather than memorising
large lists of isolated words.

### Daily Routine

| Step | Time | Task | Required output |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Recall | 5 min | Look at the Chinese meaning and recall the full English phrase | Mark each item correct, uncertain, or wrong |
| Learn | 5 min | Learn five new phrases and review three missed phrases | Notice the collocation and one common misuse |
| Use | 5 min | Write two sentences or give a 30-second spoken answer | Use at least three target phrases |

New items should mainly come from completed reading, listening, writing, and speaking
work. Learn no more than eight new items on one day.

### Weekly Schedule

| Day | Vocabulary focus |
|---|---|
| Monday | Education and the current word bank |
| Tuesday | Phrases extracted from reading |
| Wednesday | Phrases extracted from listening |
| Thursday | Writing paraphrases and collocations |
| Friday | Speaking-friendly phrases and pronunciation |
| Saturday | Mixed active-recall test; no new items |
| Sunday | Review errors and remove mastered items from the daily set |

### Spaced Review

Review each new phrase on Day 1, Day 3, Day 7, and Day 14. A phrase is considered ready
for active use only when it can be recalled from Chinese and used correctly in a new
sentence on two separate reviews.

### Four-Week Targets

| Week | Target |
|---|---|
| 1 | Master the first 10 education-related phrases and establish the review habit |
| 2 | Add 20-25 useful phrases from actual IELTS practice |
| 3 | Reuse at least 15 phrases in writing or speaking |
| 4 | Complete a mixed recall test and retain at least 80% without prompts |

Do not count words merely recognised while reading as mastered vocabulary. On Sundays,
keep wrong or uncertain items in the next week's review set and replace consistently
correct items with phrases collected from new practice.
